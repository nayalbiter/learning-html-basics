→ Independence Day ← 

In Mexico, Independence Day is celebrated starting the night of September 15, 
each governor shows to the public at midnight in their municipality or city hall 
and after showing respect to the Mexican Flag and singing the national anthem, 
they continue with a big party in honor to the national heroes. Some families 
have their own parties at home with friends and Mexican food, these parties are
called "Noche Mexicana" (Mexican Night).

The celebration continues the next day September 16, in the morning you can see
a parade in each municipality or city, where participate military, students, 
and other businesses. The most famous parade happens in Mexico City.

So, thing about the Mexican Independence Day Celebration in 3 phases:

  1. Honoring the Mexican flag and singing the national anthem
  2. Big party at nigth
  3. Parade

→ The Day of the dead ←

Dia de Muertos or Day of the Dead is a holiday celebrated on
November 2nd. Mexican people create home altars to remember friends
and family members who have died. Some families use to spend the day
and night in the cemetery together, they decorate the graves and spend
the time remembering and talking about the life of the ones who are dead.

Things that you need to create a home altar

  • candles
  • flowers
  • food
  • picture of the dead people
  • sugar and chocolate skulls
  • etc.
